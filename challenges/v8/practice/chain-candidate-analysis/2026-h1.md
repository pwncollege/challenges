## Candidate 1: CVE-2026-7337 + JSPI Suspender EPT/Native-Stack Reuse

Components:

- First stage: Maglev, Smi-check elision/type confusion.
- Second stage: Wasm JSPI, `WasmSuspenderObject`, external-pointer table entry, native stack pool.

Relevant bugs and fixes:

- CVE-2026-7337 / b/500880819: fixed by [`cc9a97f192319ed218c191fa20cbecd1bf51cb71`](https://chromium.googlesource.com/v8/v8.git/+/cc9a97f192319ed218c191fa20cbecd1bf51cb71) on 2026-04-14 in branch-heads/14.7. The mainline fix is [`b9be4febd638434a37a4215b8ea9ae1f8fab4df6`](https://chromium.googlesource.com/v8/v8.git/+/b9be4febd638434a37a4215b8ea9ae1f8fab4df6), also 2026-04-14. The patch says Maglev could drop a tagged `CheckSmi`, allowing a HeapNumber pointer to flow into consumers such as Smi-field stores.
- Introducing CL called out by the fix: [`6469250a124acef52bceabae77ed887f5c8f29ac`](https://chromium.googlesource.com/v8/v8.git/+/6469250a124acef52bceabae77ed887f5c8f29ac) on 2025-09-26.
- JSPI unwind second stage, b/501147587: fixed by [`13c7629466fa3081df011d95cf35a20ed4b7fc63`](https://chromium.googlesource.com/v8/v8.git/+/13c7629466fa3081df011d95cf35a20ed4b7fc63) on 2026-04-15. The fix clears the suspender EPT entry on exception unwind.
- Related JSPI stack-return issue, b/485784597: fixed by [`54cf5fa964f0734a8277ea2837aa2e4168e3240a`](https://chromium.googlesource.com/v8/v8.git/+/54cf5fa964f0734a8277ea2837aa2e4168e3240a) on 2026-02-25. The fix notes that, with sandbox corruption, a trusted `WasmSuspenderObject` could be kept alive and reused after its stack returned to the stack pool.

Overlap window:

- Strongest inferred overlap: 2025-09-26 through 2026-04-14, ending at the Maglev first-stage fix, assuming the JSPI unwind bug existed before its 2026-04-15 fix.
- A narrower, concrete related overlap with the stack-return variant is 2025-09-26 through 2026-02-25.

Capability path:

1. Use CVE-2026-7337 to obtain in-heap-sandbox object corruption or RW by abusing Maglev's missing tagged Smi check.
2. Use that corruption against JSPI/Wasm suspender state, keeping a trusted suspender object or its external-pointer entry reachable after stack return or exception unwind.
3. Reuse the stale EPT/native-stack reference as the heap-sandbox escape primitive.

Why it might work:

- The first-stage fix text describes a credible Maglev type confusion leading to unsafe stores.
- The second-stage fixes explicitly discuss sandbox corruption keeping a trusted suspender object alive and the need to clear an EPT entry tied to stack lifetime.
- The bugs are close in time and overlap with a known introducing commit for the first stage.

Blockers and uncertainties:

- Need to prove CVE-2026-7337 yields a sufficiently controllable write in a revision before `13c7629` or `54cf5f`.
- JSPI may require flags or feature availability in the target d8 build.
- The exact object layout and lifetime path for reliable native-stack or trusted-memory reuse still needs exploit work.

Priority: High.
